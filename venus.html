Venus

A hot planet with some spunk! They call it “Earth’s twin”, but it’s far more special than that! It’s the brightest planet in the solar system where days last for years!
Fun facts:
• Venus was the first planet to be explored by a spacecraft
• One day on venus is equal to 243 days on Earth
• Venus has more active volcanoes than any other planet in the solar system
• Venus has 90 times the air pressure of Earth

More Information

Size: Venus and Earth are similar in size. Venus has a radius of about 6,000 kilometers, while Earth has a radius of about 6,400 kilometers.
Surface: Venus’ thick atmosphere traps gases that make the surface appear in a green/yellow hue. Venus is brighter than any other planet or star in the night sky because its clouds are very reflective.
Temperature: Venus’ thick atmosphere makes it the hottest planet in our solar system. It is about 475 degrees Celsius.

A mysterious location that no one has ever been able to see in person. Maybe you will be the first to see this volcano in the flesh.
